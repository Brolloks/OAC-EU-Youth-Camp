Evangelistic Groups

Group Structure
13 groups of 4–5 youth members
Each group nominates a Chairperson responsible for organising breadbreaks
All youth must actively participate in breadbreaks
Each group is assigned a facilitating Officer or Help Power
What is Required
Prepare as if you will be personally testifying
Research both of your group's assigned topics thoroughly
Break bread together about each topic during your preparation
Your Output
Prepare a slide summarising each topic with scriptural references and explanation
One nominated youth member per group will privately portray an assigned character profile
Testify to your character regarding one of your topics at camp

Evangelistic Character Challenge

Character Profiles 1 – 5

Profile 01
The Thoughtful Scientific Thinker
Values logic and evidence. Cautious of beliefs that cannot be clearly reasoned.
Profile 02
The Cautious Churchgoer
Has past church experience. Still values faith but hesitant about involvement.
Profile 03
The Spiritual-but-Not-Religious
Open to spirituality but uncomfortable with organised religion.
Profile 04
The Friendly Skeptic
Open to conversation but sees faith as a personal choice.
Profile 05
The Busy Professional
Open to faith in principle but struggles to see how it fits daily life.
Evangelistic Character Challenge

Character Profiles 6 – 10

Profile 06
The Cultural Christian
Familiar with Christianity by tradition but does not actively practise.
Profile 07
The Moral Humanist
Values ethics and living well. Believes morality needs no religion.
Profile 08
The Curious Friend
Genuinely interested and open to faith, still exploring and not yet settled.
Profile 09
The Hurt Believer
Believes in God but hurt by past experiences with church or people.
Profile 10
The Uninterested Peer
Not opposed to faith but not engaged or curious about deeper topics.
